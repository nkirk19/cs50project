Data Citation


Data Sets

Five data sets were used in the creation of this project. They are all listed below. Please feel free to click on the links to take you to the original source of information.

1. FIPS Code

The Federal Information Processing Standards (FIPS) Code was used to standardize datasets. The FIPS code assigns a code for each county in the US. Thus, across the various datasets mentioned below, the FIPS code acted as the 'id' to link together data. Interestingly, FIPS are public standards developed and upkept by the National Institute of Standards and Technology for the purpose of cybersecurity. It was first established in 2008 by the US Department of Commerce to establish a centralized way of identifying counties across the country.

2. 2016 Presidential Election Results

Data of the results of the 2016 Presidential Election were used to investigate whether certain factors correlated with political preference. This data includes the total number of votes, total number of votes cast for the Democratic contender, Hillary Clinton, and the total number of votes cast for the Republican contender, Donald Trump.

3. Unemployment

Data regarding unemployment rates in the United States was used as one of the factors analyzed in the project. The dataset used is compiled by the United States Department of Agriculture Economic Research Service. This dataset includes the total civilian labor force count, the number of employed people, the number of unemployed people, and the unemployment rate for each county every year between 2007-2018.

4. Poverty

Data concerning poverty rates in the United States was also taken from the United States Department of Agriculture Economic Research Service. This data includes the overall poverty rate of each county in the United States in the year 2017.

5. Education

Data concerning education rates in the United States was also taken from the United States Department of Agriculture Economic Research Service. This data includes educational attainment within each county in 1970, 1980, 1990, 2000, and 2013-2017. Educational attainment levels include: 'Less than a high school diploma', 'High school diploma only', 'Some college (1-3 years), and 'Four years of college or higher'.
